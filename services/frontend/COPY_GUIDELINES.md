# ATLAS Copy Guidelines

## Tone & Voice

**Authoritative, calm, and precise.**

- No hype, no buzzwords, no marketing fluff
- Written for C-level executives, board members, senior architects, and policymakers
- Communicate power through clarity, not exaggeration
- Assume the user is intelligent and time-constrained

## Core Message

**ATLAS is not a dashboard.**
**ATLAS is not a report generator.**
**ATLAS is a strategic intelligence system that transforms global complexity into actionable decisions.**

## Writing Principles

1. **Prefer short, confident statements**
2. **Use white space and hierarchy**
3. **Every word must justify its existence**
4. **If a sentence sounds like marketing, remove it**

## Terminology

### Instead of → Use

- "Entity ID" → "Strategic Entity of Interest"
- "Assess Risk" → "Generate Strategic Risk Profile"
- "country-BRA" → "Federative Republic of Brazil"
- "Dashboard" → "Strategic Operations Center"
- "Report" → "Intelligence Summary"
- "Alert" → "Strategic Notification"
- "User" → "Operator" or "Decision Maker"

## Capability Descriptions

### Strategic Risk Assessment
"Continuous evaluation of geopolitical, economic, climate, and infrastructure risks. Multi-dimensional analysis designed to support capital allocation, expansion, and resilience decisions."

### Intelligence Analysis
"AI-driven synthesis of global open-source intelligence. Correlation of weak signals before they become crises. Explainable outputs suitable for executive review."

### Scenario & Impact Simulation
"Forward-looking simulation engine. What-if analysis across policy, supply chain, climate, and market dynamics. Decision rehearsal before real-world execution."

## Status Indicators

- "Operational" (not "Online")
- "Compliant" (not "Secure")
- "Multicloud" (not "Scalable")

## Visual Hierarchy

- Large, light fonts for hero text
- Generous white space
- Subtle borders, not heavy shadows
- Muted color palette (grays, not bright colors)
